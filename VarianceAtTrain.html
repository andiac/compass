Variance at training points¶

Let $x^*=X$, then $k_*^T=k_*=k(x_*, x_*)=K$, for noise-free observations, we have $$ \begin{align} \mathbb{V}(f_*)&= K - K^TK^{-1}K = 0 \end{align} $$ For noisy observations, $$ \begin{align} \mathbb{V}(f_*)&=K- K(K+\sigma_n^2 I )^{-1} K\\ &=K(I - (K+\sigma_n^2 I)^{-1} K)\\ &=K(K+\sigma_n^2 I)^{-1}(K + \sigma_n^2 I - K)\\ &=\sigma_n^2K(K + \sigma_n^2 I)^{-1} \end{align} $$
